INTRODUCTION
The finite difference method is a very effective way to use in the fluid flow equation for the following reasons:
  1. Derivatives in the PDE are approximated by linear combination of function vlaues at grid points.
  2. Reduces a linear/non-linear PDE into a system of algebric equations.
  3. Derived from Taylor series.
After applying the finite difference method we can discretize in both space and time, meaning we can break down continuous functions, variables, models or equations into smaller discrete parts, and in our case the discretization in space has a cell centered approach so centered difference is widely used in this scheme. In our project the main assumption was that the reservoir is a homogeneous, 1D reservoir and transient flow state (unsteady state), and the reservoir is divided into grids and the main purpose of this is just for simplicity since cubic/rectangular grids are easier to define and deal with.
